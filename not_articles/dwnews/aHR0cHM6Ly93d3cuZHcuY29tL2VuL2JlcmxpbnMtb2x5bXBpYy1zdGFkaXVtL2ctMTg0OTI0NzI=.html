    Berlin's Olympic Stadium

    It is one of the world's best-known stadiums and will be the venue for the DFB cup final on May 25. We take a look at the arena’s eventful history with a lineup of eleven pictures.

    • DFB Cup final

      Alongside the Bundesliga championships, the DFB German Cup is the Bundesliga’s second-most important title. It includes clubs from both the first and the second Bundesliga as well as qualifying clubs from lower leagues. Since 1985, the final of the DFB Cup is carried out at Berlin's Olympic Stadium. This year FC Bayern Munich and RB Leipzig will compete for the trophy.

    • Imposing geometry

      The monumental stadium is situated on a large expanse of land at the western edge of Berlin. Even today, it’s still dominated by the unadorned façade faced with dressed limestone. With its five rings, the fifteen-meter-wide Olympic gate at the entrance recalls the arena’s original purpose.

    • A propagandistic opening

      The stadium was built for the 1936 Summer Olympic Games. Adolf Hitler, who had become Reich Chancellor several years before, was keenly aware of the importance of the games and supported the arena’s construction. At the opening on August 1, he staged a major propaganda event, marching into the stadium before a cheering crowd of 110,000 spectators.

    • Post-war-era

      To this day, visitors to the adjacent Mayfield bell tower can enjoy a view of the stadium grounds and the city beyond. But this tower is a reconstruction dating from 1962 – the original structure was destroyed in the war. The original bell, which was the emblem of the 1936 Summer Games, was also destroyed. Today the tower is both a memorial and a viewing platform.

    • Hertha's home arena

      After years of economic revival in the Federal Republic of Germany, the Bundesliga soccer league was founded in 1962. Beginning with the first 1963-1964 season, the Olympic Stadium was the arena for Hertha BSC’s home matches, here shown playing their first match against FC Nuremberg. Berlin fans are traditionally seated in the eastern end of the stadium.

    • Post 2000 modernization

      After Germany got the nod for the 2006 FIFA World Cup, Berlin’s Olympic Stadium was given an overhaul. Alongside many technical renovations, all 75,000 spectator seats were covered by a roof. The surrounding athletic track was changed to blue and white, the colors of the Hertha BSC home club.

    • Stadium chapel

      During the renovations, a Christian chapel was build under the main tribune of the arena. The German national squad is said to have attended a service here before the 2006 FIFA World Cup Quarter Final match against Argentina – a match they won in a penalty shootout. But it’s not just athletes who make use of the chapel. True-blue Hertha fans can celebrate weddings and baptisms there.

    • World Championships in athletics

      In August 2009, the world’s top track and field athletes gathered in Berlin’s Olympic Stadium. About 400,000 spectators attended the nine-day event in all and millions watched at home on television. Three world records were set, as were countless national records. In 2018, the stadium will be the main site of the European Athletics Championships.

    • Venue for rock concerts

      Today the stadium is one of Europe’s most modern multifunctional arenas. Bands and musicians from all around the world appear here, including Irish rock group U2, which performed before more than 90,000 fans. Berlin’s largest venue also has excellent acoustics.

    • Behind the scenes

      Even apart from sporting events, 300,000 people visit the stadium each year to learn more about its eventful history. A multimedia guide is also available for visitors who want to learn more about the arena. Daily tours also take in areas that are normally closed to the public, including the VIP tribune, the players’ dressing rooms, and the underground warm-up hall.

    • Olympics for everyone

      If you want to take a dip during the heat of the summer, the outdoor swimming pool at the Olympic Stadium may be just the ticket. It’s part of the sports complex and was also built in 1936. It features a 50-meter pool and a diving pool with a one-meter and three-meter board.
